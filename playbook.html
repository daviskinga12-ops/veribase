Legal · Step 1

Register Veribase Limited

You must register before collecting any money or signing any contracts. This is the single most urgent action. It takes 3 working days and costs KSh 10,650.

Do this before anything else. Without a registered company you are personally liable for everything Veribase does.

Exact steps on eCitizen

  1. 1
    Go to ecitizen.go.ke and create an account with your national ID and email.
  2. 2
    Navigate to Business Registration Service → Private Limited Company. Do not choose sole proprietorship — you need limited liability protection.
  3. 3
    Run a name search for "Veribase Limited". Cost: KSh 150. If taken, alternatives: Veribase Technologies Limited, Veribase Africa Limited.
  4. 4
    Fill in the incorporation form. Registered office: use any physical Nairobi address you have access to.
  5. 5
    List yourself as sole director and shareholder initially. You can add investors as shareholders later.
  6. 6
    Pay KSh 10,500 registration fee via M-Pesa. You will receive a Certificate of Incorporation within 3 working days.
  7. 7
    Once incorporated, apply for a KRA PIN for the company at itax.kra.go.ke. This is free and required for all business transactions.

What you will receive

  • Certificate of Incorporation (your company legally exists)
  • Company Registration Number (CR Number)
  • Company KRA PIN

Total cost: approximately KSh 10,650. Total time: 3–5 working days.

Legal · Step 2

Register with the ODPC

Under the Kenya Data Protection Act, 2019, any person who processes personal data for a business purpose must register with the Office of the Data Protection Commissioner. This is mandatory. Penalties for non-compliance reach KSh 5 million.

Do not collect a single email address until this is complete. Your waitlist already has signups — complete this immediately after company registration.

Steps

  1. 1
    Go to odpc.go.ke and create an account using your company details and CR Number.
  2. 2
    Select "Register as Data Controller/Processor". Veribase is both — you control what data is collected and you process it.
  3. 3
    Complete the registration form. Describe your data processing activities accurately: "Collecting and processing business registration data, employment history, commercial transaction records, and contact information for purposes of business trust verification and economic intelligence."
  4. 4
    Pay the registration fee (currently KSh 5,000 for data processors).
  5. 5
    Receive your Data Controller/Processor registration certificate.
  6. 6
    Add your ODPC registration number to the Privacy Policy on your website.

Strategy · Step 4

Cold start strategy

Veribase is a network business. Its value grows with every member. But on day one you have zero members, zero data, and zero value to offer. This is the cold start problem — and it has killed more promising network businesses than any other challenge.

Here is the exact three-phase plan to solve it.

Phase 1 — Seed the data (Month 1–2)

Do not wait for businesses to join before there is value. Build value before the first signup.

  1. 1
    Research 500 Nairobi businesses using public records: BRS database, KRA PIN lookup, court records portal (judiciary.go.ke), business directory listings, Google Maps reviews.
  2. 2
    Build a basic profile for each: name, registration status, years in operation, address, industry, any public court cases or compliance issues.
  3. 3
    When a new business signs up, they immediately see value — they can look up any of these 500 businesses and get a real result, before contributing any data themselves.

The rule of cold start: the first user should get value even if they are the only user. Public data seeding is how you guarantee this.

Phase 2 — Anchor tenant strategy (Month 2–4)

Find one existing network and plug into it. Do not try to grow one business at a time.

Target organisations (in priority order)

  1. Kenya National Chamber of Commerce and Industry (KNCCI) — nairobi@kenyachamber.or.ke — 5,000+ business members nationwide
  2. Kenya Association of Manufacturers (KAM) — 800+ manufacturing members
  3. Association of Microfinance Institutions (AMFI) — could make Veribase mandatory for loan applicants
  4. A large SACCO — Stima SACCO, Mwalimu National, Kenya Police SACCO — each has thousands of member businesses
  5. A wholesale distributor — e.g. Unilever Kenya supplier network — mandate Veribase for all supplier onboarding

The pitch to an anchor tenant: "Give your members a free premium Veribase account for 6 months. They can look up any supplier or candidate before trusting them. You look good, they get value, we get the network."

Phase 3 — Network effects ignite (Month 4+)

Once you have 300+ businesses, something shifts. Businesses start joining because other businesses they know are already on it. The marginal cost of each new member drops. Organic word-of-mouth begins. This is the moment you have been building toward.

Signal that you have reached ignition: weekly signups start growing without any marketing spend.

Strategy · Step 5

Year 1 moat-building plan

Your moat does not exist until you build it deliberately. Here is exactly what to do in Year 1 to make Veribase permanently defensible.

Data moat

Every business interaction logged on Veribase makes the dataset more complete, more accurate, and harder to replicate. Your goal in Year 1 is to achieve a critical mass of data — 500+ businesses, 2,000+ staff records, 1,000+ supplier records — at which point the dataset becomes genuinely useful to institutions and genuinely difficult to match.

Regulatory moat

Register with the ODPC. Apply to become a recognised credit information provider under the Banking Act. Meet with the Central Bank of Kenya's financial inclusion team. Being known and trusted by regulators before competitors arrive is an enormous and durable advantage.

Institutional relationship moat

Close your first bank or fintech API deal before end of Year 1. Once a major financial institution integrates Veribase into their underwriting process, you become infrastructure they cannot easily remove. Each institutional deal makes every future deal easier.

Trust moat

Your brand reputation for accuracy and fairness is a moat. Build it by: having a clear dispute resolution process, being transparent about how data is used, and publicly correcting errors when found. A platform businesses trust is one they contribute to — and that contribution deepens the data moat.

Vercel Edge Functions · API key auth · Rate limiting · Webhooks

Core data schema

The five tables that power the MVP. Start with these in Supabase.

businesses

id · name · kra_pin · brs_number · industry · location · verified_at · subscription_status · created_at

staff_records

id · subject_name · subject_id_number · logged_by_business_id · employment_start · employment_end · exit_reason · performance_rating (1-5) · notes · is_disputed · created_at

supplier_records

id · supplier_name · supplier_id · logged_by_business_id · relationship_type · reliability_rating (1-5) · payment_behavior · notes · is_disputed · created_at

disputes

id · record_type · record_id · disputed_by_business_id · reason · evidence_url · status (open/resolved/rejected) · resolved_at · created_at

subscriptions

id · business_id · plan · mpesa_transaction_id · amount · period_start · period_end · status · created_at

Enable Supabase Row Level Security on every table from day one. businesses can only write their own records and read others'. Never skip this — it is the security foundation of the entire platform.
